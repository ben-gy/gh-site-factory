## Architecture Decisions

**Vanilla TS over React.** One view switcher plus two drill-down panels; a component tree would be weight
for nothing.

**Nine views, chosen for this data's shape.** Deliberately *no* force-directed network graph: the data is
a bipartite commodityâ†”partner flow with ~350 Ă— ~260 nodes, and a dot-cloud would be strictly less legible
than the Sankey and matrix that already encode those relationships. Form followed the data, not the house
default.

**Natural Earth 50m map units, not 110m countries.** 110m drops Singapore and Bahrain entirely and folds
Hong Kong into China â€” and Hong Kong is Australia's 5th-largest export destination ($22.5bn). Map units at
50m gives 265 features and its own Hong Kong polygon. Simplified with mapshaper at 20% (368 KB shipped;
China 646 vertices, Indonesia 947 â€” real coastlines). 211 of 220 real partners join to a shape; the 9 that
don't are tiny island territories worth $27m combined.

**Log colour scale on the map, not quantiles.** This was a correction made after looking at the first
render: with quantiles over 224 partners, China ($185bn) and Canada ($2bn) landed in the same darkest
bucket and the map hid the single most important fact about Australian trade. Log breaks make the
concentration visible. Balance mode uses symmetric log breaks around zero.

**The confidentiality model is the product.** See below â€” it drove the Exposure view's hollow bubbles, the
Blind Spots view, the `renderPartnerCell` "mostly withheld" chip, and the published-destinations-only
denominator throughout.

## Data Modelling â€” the traps found in the source

1. **Suppression is level-dependent.** At `COMMODITY_SITC=TOT` the country split is essentially complete
   (unattributed 1.6%). At division level it is not: **gas is 96.3% `NCD`**. Verified directly against the
   API before designing anything. Consequence: commodityĂ—country data cannot be treated as complete, and
   concentration must be computed over published destinations only.
2. **`NCD` "No Country Details" is not a country** â€” nor are `SHIP`, `CNAV`, `UNKN`, `AFZ`, `ANCA`,
   `AUST` (re-imports). Ranking them produces "Australia's largest gas customer is No Country Details".
3. **Country aggregates overlap** (`OECD`, `ASEAN`, `APEC`, `EURO27`, `DC`, `LDC`, `JPDA`) â€” excluded from
   every ranking.
4. **SITC 98 "combined confidential items"** is a confidentiality bucket, not a product, and is large
   enough ($4.44bn) to appear in a naive top-commodity list. Flagged everywhere.
5. **Values are AUD thousands** (`UNIT_MULT=3`) â€” converted once at parse time.
6. **Import history starts July 2000; exports start July 1996.**

## Bugs caught by looking at the rendered output

These are the ones that would have shipped silently:

1. **`, nes` regex corrupted words containing "nes".** `"Office machines and automatic data processing
   machines"` rendered as `"Office machi and automatic data processing"`. The qualifier is only ever
   trailing â€” anchored the regex and added a regression test covering machines/engines/pipelines.
2. **Four years of fabricated $0 imports.** ABS imports start July 2000; zero-filling drew Australia
   importing exactly nothing from 1996â€“2000, and it looked entirely plausible. Added `densifyNullable`,
   made the trend chart break the line on null, and the tooltip say "imports not published before July
   2000".
3. **Quantile map shading hid the concentration** (see above).
4. **Tooltips printed raw HTML.** The canonical `patterns/tooltip.ts` sets `textContent`; my tips carried
   `<strong>`/`<br>`. Converted every tip to plain text with newlines + `white-space: pre-line`. Also the
   CSS had no `.hover-tip { display: none }` / `.visible` rule at all, so the tooltip never hid.
5. **Colliding labels on the scatter** â€” four big exports cluster at $10â€“100bn / ~30%. Added greedy
   label placement with collision avoidance (drops a label rather than overlapping) plus positional tests.
6. **Colliding annotation labels** on the trend chart in the 2020â€“2023 cluster â€” replaced two-row
   alternation with row-packing against occupied extents.
7. **"Largest buyer: China 53%" on natural gas** in the commodity table â€” technically the top published
   destination, but exactly the misleading claim this site exists to avoid. Now renders a "mostly
   withheld" chip when suppression exceeds 50%, and an asterisk with the withheld share above 5%.
8. **Exposure view overflowed horizontally at 375px** â€” the scatter wasn't inside a scroller. Wrapped it
   and reparented the zoom controls to the positioned outer element so they stay pinned.
9. **Bar labels clipped with no ellipsis** â€” `text-overflow` has no effect on a flex container.
10. **`.dd-stat span` matched nested spans**, breaking the glossary link onto its own line.
