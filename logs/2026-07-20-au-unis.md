# Build Log: Universities
**Date:** 2026-07-20
**Status:** deployed

## Idea Source

Researched. `IDEAS.md` was empty, so I looked for an Australian public-data domain the
fleet had not touched. Education was almost untouched — `au-childcare` covers early
learning and nothing covers tertiary at all — and the Department of Education publishes
a genuinely deep annual collection that nobody can read.

I checked data availability before committing to the idea rather than after: queried the
data.gov.au CKAN entry (a dead link to a landing page), then scraped
`education.gov.au/higher-education-statistics/student-data` directly, downloaded seven
workbooks, and inspected their structure. Only once I had confirmed per-institution
attrition series, per-institution student-staff ratios, a full origin-destination matrix
and a nine-year cohort completion decomposition did I write the plan.

Rejected alternatives: ABS building approvals by LGA (too close to `au-planning`'s
housing/development domain), Safe Work Australia fatalities (thinner analytically),
NGER corporate emissions (adjacent to `au-pollution`).

## Site Details
- **Name:** Universities
- **Repo:** ben-gy/au-unis
- **Category:** education
- **Audience:** Year 12 students and their parents (mobile, December, anxious, arriving from a Google search about one university and one number); international students weighing institutions during the visa squeeze; higher-ed journalists and policy staff
- **Stack:** Vite 6 + vanilla TypeScript + Leaflet + Vitest
- **Data strategy:** pipeline. Source publishes **annually** (student data ~September, staff ~March), so the cron is **yearly** — `23 19 9 10 *`, 9 October, after the September release settles. Anything faster would re-download identical workbooks.

Download URLs embed Drupal node IDs that change every release, so the collector walks
the stable index pages, picks the newest year carrying the sections it needs, and
scrapes each resource page for its actual `/download/…` href. The staff index only lists
per-year landing pages, so it follows one extra hop.

## Architecture Decisions

**Vanilla TS, not React.** This is a view switcher over one dataset with no routing and
no deep component tree. React would have added weight for nothing.

**A dependency-free pipeline.** I wrote a minimal ZIP reader (`zip.mjs`, using Node's
`zlib`) and XLSX reader (`xlsx.mjs`) rather than pulling in a spreadsheet library. That
removes `npm install` from the data workflow entirely — a whole class of CI failure gone
for what is, in the end, "read some XML out of a zip".

**Hand-rolled SVG for every chart.** Treemap and zoom come from `patterns/`; bars,
Sankey, lines, histogram and the stacked cohort chart are hand-rolled. No D3.

**No force-directed network and no fine-grained map, deliberately.** Institutions do not
connect to each other in this data, so a graph would be decoration; and there is no
geography below "state of the institution", so anything more specific would be inventing
detail the source does not contain. The view set follows the data's three real shapes:
~60 entities with parallel 10-year series, one 9×10 origin-destination matrix, and a
cohort decomposing four ways across three horizons.

## Per-view UX critique

| View | Question it answers | Hover | Click | Next action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rankings** (default) | How does my shortlist compare, and is this number unusual? | Exact value, rank, national median | Opens the drill-down | Change measure, filter state, open a profile |
| **Who finishes** (signature) | Do people like me finish? | Band %, cohort years, plus an explicit "still studying — not a drop-out" note | Segments are clickable marks; the timeframe toggle is the real interaction | Change cohort attribute or timeframe; the ATAR strip below invites comparison |
| **Exposure** | Which universities are most exposed to a visa-policy shock, and how big are they? | Total/overseas/domestic split | Opens the drill-down | Zoom/pan; open a profile |
| **Origins** | Where do students come from, and which states import them? | Exact flow and share; map shows net and ratio | Map click filters Rankings by state; hovering a node isolates its ribbons | Switch Flow/Map/Matrix |
| **Study areas** | What does Australia study, and what is this university *for*? | Enrolments and share | Treemap cell re-sorts the matrix by that field; matrix row opens the profile | Drill into a university |
| **Trends** | Is this a trend or a blip? | Per-year values; annotations explain the two inflections | Rows open the drill-down | Switch national/by-university, change measure |
| **Explorer** | Show me everything about everyone | Full 10-year series in the sparkline tooltip | Row opens the drill-down | Sort, search, filter |
| **Insights** | What should I have noticed? | Histogram bin contents | Card opens its source view; bar lists the universities in that bin, each a button | Follow the click-through |

Every visually-interactive element was walked and wired — no dead clicks. Legends are
explicitly `cursor: default` where they are not interactive, so nothing looks clickable
that isn't.

**What a domain expert would change, and what I did about it:** the obvious league-table
instinct is to rank universities by completion rate. The department does not publish
completion by institution (only by cohort attribute nationally), and pretending otherwise
would be fabrication — so the site ranks what exists and puts the cohort analysis in its
own view rather than forcing it into a ranking.

## Data traps handled

All eight confirmed in the raw files; each produces a confidently wrong number if ignored.

1. **Aggregate rows share the institution column** — `National Total`, `Table A/B Providers`, `State Total`, `Total 2023`, and a per-state `Non-University Higher Education Institutions` bucket. The build asserts the institution total (1,478,800) is *less* than the national total (1,676,077) by a plausible margin, so a leak would fail the build.
2. **String sentinels in numeric columns** — `np`, `< 5`, `.` → `null`, never `0`.
3. **Structural zeros in rate series** — Avondale reads 0 attrition for nine years then 12.65. Left alone it is the best university in Australia.
4. **Sector vs provider attrition** — different denominators; never share an axis, each labelled with its basis.
5. **The four-year completion trap** — defaults to nine years; "still enrolled" coloured neutral.
6. **Footnote markers and codes welded into names**, inconsistently between workbooks.
7. **Three-row merged headers** with two metric blocks side by side in the staff workbooks.
8. **Footnote paragraphs written into data columns** in Section 17 and the time series.

Ninth, in the frontend: the interstate map excludes overseas students on purpose.

## Bugs found and fixed during the build

1. **XLSX reader silently corrupted sparse rows.** A greedy `[^>]*` attribute run swallowed the `/` of a self-closing `<c r="D3" s="41"/>`, matched the following `>`, and consumed every cell up to the next `</c>` — so an *empty* cell stole the value of the next non-empty one and shifted the rest of the row. It parsed without error and was quietly wrong. Fixed by parsing attributes as explicit `name="value"` pairs; same bug class fixed in the `<row>`, `<sheet>` and `<Relationship>` patterns.
2. **Header located by substring matched the title row.** Section 2.4's *title* contains the phrase "State of Permanent Home Residence", so the header search selected row 2 and the origins grand total came out 0. Fixed with an exact match; the test suite documents it.
3. **The page never left its loading skeleton.** `lastRenderKey` was initialised to `''`, and an empty hash produces an empty view key, so the first render was a no-op. Now a `null` sentinel.
4. **`&#10;` rendered literally in every SVG tooltip.** `setAttribute` takes a raw string and the HTML parser never sees it, so entity references are not decoded. Split into `tipText` (for `setAttribute`, real newlines) and `tipAttr` (for `innerHTML` interpolation, escaped + `&#10;`), audited all 28 call sites, and added tests for both.
5. **Scatter labels landed on their own and neighbouring dots.** Added dots as obstacles, then found strict avoidance dropped the most important labels — so each label now offers four candidate corners (`placeLabelsMulti`). 13 of 18 place cleanly with no overlaps.
6. **The interstate map was uniformly red.** Including overseas students makes every state an importer, so the choropleth said nothing. Switched to domestic-only, which discriminates properly (Tasmania 1.47 vs Queensland 0.87) and documented why in the view's own subtitle.
7. **Horizontal overflow at 375px on the Outcomes view.** A `<select>` sizes itself to its longest option ("Australian Tertiary Admission Rank (ATAR) - for school leavers"), pushing the page to 407px. Fixed with `max-width: 100%` on `.control-group` plus `min-width: 0` on the select.
8. **The data pipeline failed on CI.** `education.gov.au` returned connect timeouts (not a 403) from GitHub-hosted runners. Fixed with a full browser header set including `Sec-Fetch-*` and `dns.setDefaultResultOrder('ipv4first')` — runners advertise IPv6 and the host only half-answers on it. Verified working: the CI run now collects all seven workbooks and reproduces the national total exactly. The collector also now distinguishes *unreachable* from *changed*, so a future network block leaves committed data untouched and exits clean rather than producing a red X that re-running cannot fix.
