## Idea Source
Researched. `IDEAS.md` was empty (no lines starting with `-`), so I researched a new idea.

Registry coverage of AU government data is already broad (crime, hospitals, aged care, childcare, charities, pollution, dams, jobs, pay gap, contracts, gov org, pokies, donations, road crashes, planning, flights, visas, immunisation, super, treaties, MP expenses, NDIS), so the bar was a genuinely new domain rather than another angle on an existing one.

Candidates weighed: PBS prescription dispensing, ATO taxation statistics by postcode, ACARA school performance, and CER rooftop solar postcode data. Chose rooftop solar because it is (a) a domain with nothing in the registry, (b) highly searchable ("solar panels [suburb]"), (c) geographic and per-capita-normalisable, (d) topical — the federal Cheaper Home Batteries rebate started 1 July 2025 and the battery data begins that exact month, and (e) it supports an analytical angle nobody else publishes.

**Expansion check:** not an expansion of `nemwatch` (live NEM spot prices/demand — different data, cadence and audience) or `au-dams` (BOM storage levels). Different data shape, different audience, new domain. Built as a new site.

## Site Details
- **Name:** Rooftop Solar
- **Repo:** ben-gy/au-solar
- **Category:** energy
- **Audience:** Australian homeowners and renters checking their own suburb (mostly mobile, arriving from search); energy journalists and policy analysts needing per-postcode penetration with a real denominator (desktop).
- **Stack:** Vanilla TypeScript + Vite 6 + Leaflet 1.9 + Vitest. Hand-rolled SVG charts, no chart library.
- **Data strategy:** pipeline, **monthly cron** (`23 5 9 * *`). The CER republishes the SRES postcode data monthly ("as at 31 May 2026"), so monthly is proportional to the source and is the fastest cadence the factory allows. Staggered to day 9, 05:23 UTC.

## Architecture Decisions

**Why join to the census at all.** The CER data alone is a 188-column wide matrix of counts. Raw counts are close to useless for a reader — they mostly rank postcodes by population. The denominator (ABS G37 `Total_Total`, 9,275,066 occupied private dwellings) is what makes it a per-home rate. G37 was chosen over G01 specifically because it cross-tabulates **tenure × dwelling structure**, which yields `O_OR_DS_Sep_house + O_MTG_DS_Sep_house` — owner-occupied detached houses, i.e. the households that actually control a roof. That single derived field is what the site's signature view is built on.

**Why a scatter, not just a map.** Design research: APVI's pv-map is the domain incumbent and already shows penetration on a map — it never explains *why* a postcode is low. Clearing that bar meant leading with explanation, so the Solar Divide scatter (ownership vs uptake, fitted trend, R²) is the signature view and the map is supporting.

**Vanilla TS over React:** single page, eight hash-routed views, no deep component tree.

**Leaflet with `preferCanvas: true`:** 2,640 polygons as individual SVG paths pans badly.

**Boundaries:** downloaded from ABS in the pipeline, simplified with mapshaper at 1.2% (`keep-shapes`, precision 0.001) → 2,640 features, ~180k vertices, 1.85 MB raw / **360 KB gzipped**. Real coastlines. Never hand-authored.

**Patterns copied, not re-rolled:** `tooltip.ts`, `svgZoom.ts`, `leafletMap.ts` (adapted), `tests/layout.test.ts` (adapted). `treemap.ts` was copied but the data has no hierarchy worth a treemap, so it was **deleted rather than shipped as dead code**.

## Data gotchas found and handled

1. **Quoted thousands separators (severe).** 1,077 of 2,811 rows in the solar CSV contain a field like `"1,234"`. A naive `line.split(',')` shifts every column after the first quoted value. This silently understated the national total by **89% — 477,892 vs the true 4,437,269**. Caught during research by sanity-checking against the known ~4.4M figure. Fixed with a proper RFC4180 parser (`pipeline/lib/csv.mjs`), unit tested.
2. **Trailing months are incomplete.** STCs can be created up to 12 months after installation, so the newest months are under-reported and revise upward. May 2026 shows 8,992 batteries against April's 68,598 — paperwork, not a collapse. `detectIncompleteMonths` flags them; the UI hatches them and excludes them from headline figures.
3. **2021 denominator vs 2026 numerator.** 103 postcodes report >100 systems per 100 homes. Investigated: most are greenfield growth corridors (Angle Vale, Buckland Park), but some are small rural/industrial postcodes where farm and business rooftops carry systems no household lives under (the SRES covers commercial systems up to 100 kW — hence 3061 Campbellfield averaging 20 kW). Initially labelled all of them "growth corridors", which was wrong for the rural set; copy was corrected to cover both causes. Flagged with ⚠ everywhere, never dropped.
4. **Header naming varies across CER files** (`Installation Quantity` vs `Installations Quantity` vs `Rated Power Output in kW` vs `Usable capacity in kWh`; three different postcode column names). Month columns are matched generically on the `<Mon> <YYYY>` prefix.
5. **Null/pseudo postcodes:** POA `2043, 9494, 9797, ZZZZ` have null geometry; CER emits `0000`. Filtered.

## Bugs found during verification (all fixed)

1. **Sticky header broken by `overflow-x: hidden`.** `#app { overflow-x: hidden }` computes `overflow-y` to `auto`, making `#app` a scroll container, which silently breaks `position: sticky` on the header (measured `headerTop: -313.5` at scroll). Fixed with `overflow-x: clip`, which contains overflow without creating a scroll container. Verified `headerTop: 0` at max scroll.
2. **Drill-down reported rank #1603 for the highest-uptake postcode in the country.** `rankable()` filters but does not sort; the drawer ranked against the unsorted array. Fixed by extracting a single `rankedByPer100()` helper used by both `data.ts` and the drawer, with 5 regression tests.
3. **About modal rendered underneath the sticky nav bar.** `--z-header: 2200` beat `--z-modal: 2100`. The header must sit above Leaflet (1000) but below the overlay. Reordered to header 1500 < overlay 2000 < modal 2100. Verified by opening the modal **from the map view** and screenshotting.
4. **Map framing.** `fitBounds(layer.getBounds())` included Christmas (105°E) and Norfolk (168°E) Islands, stranding Australia in empty ocean. Fixed to a mainland+Tasmania bounds constant.
5. **Hardcoded prose contradicted the data.** The rankings subtitle claimed "Queensland and South Australia lead; the ACT and NT trail" while the data shows SA leads (66.3) and Tasmania trails (30.4). Now derived from the data at render time.
6. **Localities were wrong-looking.** Picking the alphabetically-first suburb labelled 4670 "Abbotsford" (it's Bundaberg) and 4350 "Athol" (Toowoomba). Fixed with `pickPrimaryLocality`: prefer a stem shared by 3+ suburbs (Bundaberg Central/East/North/South/West → "Bundaberg"), then the SA3/LGA name, then alphabetical. Note the SA3 alone was insufficient and actively misleading — 4670's SA3 is "Burnett", which would have picked "Burnett Downs". 9 unit tests.
7. **Drawer stayed open when switching views.** `show()` now closes it.
8. Dead code (`squarify.ts`) removed before commit.
