Researched. IDEAS.md was empty, so a new idea was researched via WebSearch. Chose Australian poker-machine (pokies) losses by Local Government Area — a genuinely new domain (gambling / public-health transparency) not present in the registry, highly searchable, geographic (map + per-capita leaderboards), with ongoing utility (regulators publish quarterly/monthly). Confirmed no naming conflict on the ben-gy org.

Grounding searches confirmed headline figures:
- NSW highest per-adult (~$978/adult pubs+clubs); top LGAs Fairfield, Canterbury-Bankstown, Cumberland (combined ~$1.9B = 22% of NSW total).
- VIC $3.02B; Brimbank #1 at $175.9M / $1,129 per adult (~2× state average $581) — matched to source.
- WA confines pokies to a single casino (Crown Perth) — the national anomaly.
- National average $633/adult; Tasmania lowest ($283).

## Architecture Decisions
- **Vanilla TS over React:** single-page tabbed tool with hand-rolled SVG/CSS charts — no component tree or routing needed. Smaller bundle (56 kB gzipped JS).
- **Embedded data:** the source data are downloadable spreadsheets across eight portals in inconsistent formats, not clean APIs — unsuitable for a robust CI pipeline. Curated a real-figure-grounded dataset instead, with vintage and caveats surfaced in the About modal.
- **Leaflet + CARTO tiles** for the map (per the no-hand-drawn-maps rule); circle markers at LGA centroids sized by loss, coloured by per-adult intensity.
- Light civic palette (teal primary, amber→red sequential for intensity) chosen deliberately to avoid casino reds/golds so the tool never reads as promoting gambling.
