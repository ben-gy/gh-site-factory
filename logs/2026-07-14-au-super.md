# Build Log: Super Funds

## Idea Source
Researched. IDEAS.md was empty. The registry is heavy on AU government datasets (visas,
foreign relations, jobs, pokies, pay gap, contracts, gov org register, flights, childcare,
charities, pollution, NDIS, aged care, crime, planning, crashes, donations, hospitals, energy,
dams). Searched for untapped, highly-searchable public datasets with real ongoing utility.
Superannuation stood out: every working Australian has it, it's intensely searchable ("is my
super fund good"), and APRA publishes rich fund-level data that nobody reads because it ships as a
40-column spreadsheet. No existing site covers super. Chose the APRA MySuper Comprehensive Product
Performance Package (CPPP) as the source.

## Architecture Decisions
- **Vanilla TS** — single-page comparison tool, no routing/component-tree complexity; keeps the
  bundle small (58KB JS / 20KB CSS).
- **No map** — super funds have no geography, so a map was deliberately omitted (per the "design
  for the data's shape" rule) in favour of a fees-vs-returns scatter as the flagship.
- **Pipeline over embedded** — the data refreshes annually; a pipeline keeps it current without a
  code change. Data committed to `public/data/` so the first build/tests have data.
- **CSV parsing gotcha:** the source has quoted fields with embedded commas (e.g. a category named
  "Resource Super & Nationwide"), so a naive split mis-columns every row. Wrote a proper RFC-4180
  parser in `pipeline/aggregate.mjs`.
- **Lifecycle products:** 24 of 52 products are lifecycle (a header row with product-level
  assets/pass-fail + one row per age stage carrying that stage's returns/fees). Headline figures
  resolve to the ACCUMULATION stage (highest growth allocation) — what most working members
  experience — while every stage is exposed in the drill-down. Documented in the glossary/About.
- **All passed:** in 2025 every MySuper product passed (values are only Pass / Pass*), so the
  framing is the *margin over benchmark*, not a wall of failures. Pass* = insufficient history to
  assess; surfaced as its own insight.
- Copied canonical patterns (`treemap.ts`, `tooltip.ts`, `svgZoom.ts`, `tests/layout.test.ts`)
  rather than re-rolling.

## Per-view UX critique (recorded)
- **Explorer** — Q: where does my fund sit? Hover: fee-in-dollars + exact assets. Click: drill-down. Next: sort/filter.
- **Rankings** — Q: who's best/worst on X? Hover: product + metric. Click: drill-down. Colour vs median.
- **Fees vs Returns** — Q: am I paying for performance? Hover: full stats. Click: drill-down. Drag pans, click doesn't fire on drag (verified). Sweet-spot quadrant shaded.
- **Fee Heatmap** — Q: does my fund get cheaper as my balance grows? Hover: %/$ per tier. Click: drill-down. Sorted cheapest-first.
- **Trustees** — Q: who runs the money? Hover: assets/members/return. Click: drill-down. Squarified, per-trustee colour, group labels.
- **Distribution** — Q: is my fund an outlier? Hover: bin range + count. Metric toggle + stat tiles + median marker.
- **Insights** — Q: what stands out? Every item click-throughs to its product.
