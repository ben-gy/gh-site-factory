# Expansion Ideas

Ideas for improving existing sites with new data, views, or features. These are NOT new site ideas — they're enhancements to products we've already built. The scheduled task logs these here instead of building duplicate sites.

Review periodically and fold the best ones into the existing repos.

- **au-gov**: User idea "Machinery of Government (AU)" (from IDEAS.md, 2026-07-13) overlaps the deployed au-gov (AGOR federal explorer: 1,381 bodies, 17 portfolios, timeline). The genuinely new parts to fold in as enhancements rather than a duplicate site: (a) **state/territory government structures** — each state's departments/agencies from state government directories (e.g. NSW nsw.gov.au/departments-and-agencies, VIC vic.gov.au, service directories on data.gov.au); (b) **MoG change history** — how Administrative Arrangements Orders (AAOs) have renamed/merged/split federal departments over time (PM&C publishes AAOs at pmc.gov.au/government/administrative-arrangements-orders; a department "family tree" / genealogy timeline view would be a killer feature); (c) **ministers per portfolio** from directory.gov.au (Government Online Directory has a data feed at directory.gov.au/reports/data-files). Any of these could ship as new views inside au-gov.
- **au-pollution**: Add **greenhouse gas emissions** alongside the National Pollutant Inventory's toxic substances. The Clean Energy Regulator publishes NGER (National Greenhouse and Energy Reporting) data for every large emitting facility in Australia — scope 1 and scope 2 emissions in tonnes CO2-e plus energy produced and consumed, annually and free: https://cer.gov.au/markets/reports-and-data/national-greenhouse-and-energy-reporting-data. The **Safeguard Mechanism** dataset adds each covered facility's baseline and whether it exceeded it, which is the newsworthy angle (who is over their limit, and by how much): https://cer.gov.au/schemes/safeguard-mechanism/safeguard-facility-reported-emissions. Considered as a standalone site on 2026-07-22 and rejected under the expansion rule — it is the same *shape* as au-pollution (facility-level emissions, map, treemap, rankings) and would read as "the same site with different numbers". As views inside au-pollution it is genuinely additive: a CO2-e mode on the existing facility map, a baseline-vs-actual column, and a cross-reference of which NPI facilities are also Safeguard-covered (join on ABN/facility name).
- **au-contracts**: The deployed site covers FY2023-24 + FY2024-25 (period 2023-07-01 → 2025-06-30) with a consulting-spend and tender-method focus. The just-completed **FY2025-26** (1 Jul 2025 – 30 Jun 2026) is now fully available from the AusTender OCDS API and is NOT in the site — the pipeline window is fixed and won't roll forward on its own. Enhancement: add FY2025-26 (roughly 57k contracts / ~$87B) so the year-over-year view runs through the newest year, and make the pipeline window advance automatically each 1 July (e.g. "most recent complete FY"). API: `https://api.tenders.gov.au/ocds/findByDates/contractPublished/{startISO}/{endISO}` — paginate via `links.next` cursor, dedupe amendments by base CN number (strip `-A\d+` suffix), classify by UNSPSC 2-digit segment. (A duplicate single-year FY2025-26 build was attempted on 2026-07-12 after a worktree recycle; folded here instead of shipping a second site.)
